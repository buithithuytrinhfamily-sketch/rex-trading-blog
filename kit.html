The night I stopped hunting trades and built a back office

The night after my fourth account died, I sat with my company’s books on one screen and my trading history on the other. Ten years running that business, and it never spent a dollar I hadn’t approved. Every invoice. Every coffee machine.

My chart had no such rule. My chart spent whatever my pulse told it to.

That night I saw it: the problem was never the trades. It was the operating system. So I built a back office instead.

A risk ceiling — a spending limit no single position can breach. Cost per trade, calculated before entry, like a contract read before signing. A trade journal, because a business without books isn’t a business. Three questions every night to close the books. And one rule above it all: protect your capital first. Opportunities will always come.

A losing trade stopped being a crisis. It became a line item.

Now my 9 p.m. looks like this: journal open, one win, one stop-loss hit, recorded as one approved expense. Laptop closed. Dinner. Sleep.
